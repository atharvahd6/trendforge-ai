Immigration to the United States

Work Visas (H-1B, L-1, O-1)

  • Passport (valid for at least 6 months beyond intended stay)
  • Degree certificates (evaluated by a credential evaluation service if foreign)
  • Experience letters (from previous employers, detailing job responsibilities and duration)
  • Sponsorship documents from a U.S. employer (Form I-129 for H-1B, L-1 petition)
  • Labor Condition Application (LCA) for H-1B visas
  • Proof of specialty occupation (for H-1B)

Family Sponsorship

  • Proof of relationship (birth certificates for children, marriage certificates for spouses)
  • Sponsor's income proof (Form I-864, Affidavit of Support, tax returns)
  • Medical examination records (Form I-693, completed by an approved civil surgeon)
  • Police certificates (from all countries where the applicant has lived for 6+ months since age 16)
  • Passport-style photographs (as per USCIS specifications)

Diversity Visa Lottery

  • Passport (valid for at least 6 months beyond intended entry to the U.S.)
  • Birth certificate (with English translation if necessary)
  • Police certificates (from all countries where the applicant has lived for 6+ months since age 16)
  • Medical examination records (Form I-693, completed by an approved civil surgeon)
  • Proof of high school education or equivalent, or two years of work experience in a qualifying occupation
  • Diversity Visa Lottery confirmation page (from the official DV program website)

Immigration to Canada

Express Entry

  • Language proficiency test results (IELTS, CELPIP, or TEF, taken within the last 2 years)
  • Educational credential assessment (ECA) from a designated organization (e.g., WES)
  • Proof of work experience (reference letters from employers, detailing job duties and duration)
  • Police certificates (from all countries where the applicant has lived for 6+ months since age 18)
  • Proof of funds (bank statements showing sufficient settlement funds)
  • Passport (valid for the duration of the application process)
  • Express Entry profile number and Job Seeker validation code

Family Sponsorship

  • Proof of relationship (birth certificates, marriage certificates, or proof of common-law partnership)
  • Sponsor's income proof (Notice of Assessment from the Canada Revenue Agency)
  • Medical examination records (completed by a panel physician approved by IRCC)
  • Police certificates (from all countries where the applicant has lived for 6+ months since age 18)
  • Passport (valid for the duration of the application process)
  • Proof of Canadian citizenship or permanent residency of the sponsor

Provincial Nominee Programs (PNPs)

  • Educational certificates (with ECA if foreign)
  • Work experience letters (from employers, detailing job responsibilities and duration)
  • Language test results (IELTS, CELPIP, or TEF, taken within the last 2 years)
  • Proof of funds (bank statements showing sufficient settlement funds)
  • Provincial nomination certificate
  • Job offer letter (if applicable, from a Canadian employer)
  • Proof of ties to the province (e.g., previous work or study in the province)

Immigration to Australia

General Skilled Migration (GSM)

  • Skill assessment from a relevant assessing authority (positive assessment in the nominated occupation)
  • English language proficiency test results (IELTS, TOEFL iBT, PTE Academic, or OET, taken within the last 3 years)
  • Educational certificates (with certified copies)
  • Proof of work experience (employment reference letters detailing job duties and duration)
  • Expression of Interest (EOI) submission confirmation
  • State or territory nomination (if applicable)
  • Health examination results (completed by a panel physician approved by the Australian government)
  • Police certificates (from all countries where the applicant has lived for 12+ months in the last 10 years)

Employer-Sponsored Visas

  • Sponsorship documents from the Australian employer (approved sponsorship status)
  • Nomination documents (approved nomination for the applicant's position)
  • Passport (valid for the duration of the visa)
  • Skill assessment (if required for the nominated occupation)
  • English language proficiency test results (if required)
  • Health examination results (completed by a panel physician approved by the Australian government)
  • Police certificates (from all countries where the applicant has lived for 12+ months in the last 10 years)

Family Sponsorship

  • Proof of relationship (birth certificates, marriage certificates, or proof of de facto relationship)
  • Sponsor's income proof (evidence of financial capacity to support the applicant)
  • Medical examination records (completed by a panel physician approved by the Australian government)
  • Police certificates (from all countries where the applicant has lived for 12+ months in the last 10 years)
  • Proof of Australian citizenship, permanent residency, or eligible New Zealand citizenship of the sponsor
