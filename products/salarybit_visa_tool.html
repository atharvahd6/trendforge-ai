Work Visas for the United States

Target Destination: United States

Visa Types:

  • H-1B Visa for specialty occupations
  • L-1 Visa for intracompany transferees
  • O-1 Visa for individuals with extraordinary abilities

Core Documentation

  1. Petition Approval: Approval notice from U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS) for the specific visa type.
  2. Valid Passport: A passport with at least six months' validity beyond the planned departure date from the U.S.
  3. Visa Application: Completion of the DS-160 form and payment of the application fee.
  4. Supporting Documents: Depending on the visa type, this may include diplomas, transcripts, letters from current or prospective employers, and proof of qualifications.

Structural Visa Compliance Paths

  • Employer Sponsorship: Many work visas require a U.S. employer to sponsor the applicant.
  • Labor Condition Application (LCA): For H-1B visas, the employer must file an LCA with the U.S. Department of Labor.

Study Visas for Canada

Target Destination: Canada

Visa Types:

  • Study Permit for students
  • Temporary Resident Visa (TRV) or Electronic Travel Authorization (eTA) for entry into Canada

Core Documentation

  1. Letter of Acceptance: From a designated learning institution in Canada.
  2. Proof of Funds: Demonstrating sufficient financial resources to support oneself during the study period.
  3. Passport: A valid passport with sufficient validity.
  4. Study Permit Application: Submission of the application through the Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) portal or a paper application, depending on the applicant's situation.

Structural Visa Compliance Paths

  • Designated Learning Institution (DLI): The educational institution must be a DLI for the study permit application to be valid.
  • Biometrics: Applicants may need to provide biometrics as part of the application process.

Skilled Migration Visas for Australia

Target Destination: Australia

Visa Types:

  • Skilled Independent Visa (Subclass 189)
  • Skilled Nominated Visa (Subclass 190)
  • Skilled Regional (Provisional) Visa (Subclass 491)

Core Documentation

  1. Skills Assessment: Relevant to the applicant's occupation, from a recognized assessing authority.
  2. Expression of Interest (EOI): Submission through the SkillSelect system, outlining the applicant's skills, qualifications, and experience.
  3. Invitation to Apply: Receipt of an invitation to apply for the visa after the EOI has been assessed.
  4. Health and Character Checks: Applicants must undergo health examinations and provide police certificates.

Structural Visa Compliance Paths

  • Points System: Applicants are assessed based on a points system that considers factors like age, English language proficiency, qualifications, and work experience.
  • Nomination or Sponsorship: For certain visas, applicants need to be nominated by an Australian state or territory government agency or sponsored by an eligible relative.
