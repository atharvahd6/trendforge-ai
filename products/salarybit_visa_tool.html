Immigration to the United States

The primary pathways for immigration to the U.S. include:

  • Work Visas (H-1B, L-1, O-1): For skilled workers, intra-company transferees, and individuals with extraordinary abilities.
  • Family Sponsorship: U.S. citizens and permanent residents can sponsor family members for immigration.
  • Diversity Visa Lottery: An annual lottery offering a limited number of visas to individuals from underrepresented countries.

Core Documentation:

  • Passport
  • Birth certificate
  • Marriage certificate (if applicable)
  • Divorce or death certificate (if applicable)
  • Police certificate
  • Medical examination
  • Proof of financial support
  • Educational and work experience documents

Immigration to Canada

Key pathways include:

  • Express Entry: A points-based system for skilled workers.
  • Provincial Nominee Programs (PNPs): For workers with skills needed by specific provinces.
  • Family Sponsorship: Canadian citizens and permanent residents can sponsor family members.

Essential Documents:

  • Passport
  • Birth certificate
  • Marriage certificate (if applicable)
  • Divorce or death certificate (if applicable)
  • Police certificate
  • Medical examination
  • Language test results (English or French)
  • Educational assessment report (ECA)
  • Proof of funds

Immigration to Australia

Primary pathways include:

  • Skilled Independent Visa (Subclass 189): For skilled workers who do not require sponsorship.
  • Skilled Nominated Visa (Subclass 190): For skilled workers nominated by an Australian state or territory.
  • Family Sponsorship: Australian citizens, permanent residents, and eligible New Zealand citizens can sponsor family members.

Core Documentation:

  • Passport
  • Birth certificate
  • Marriage certificate (if applicable)
  • Divorce or death certificate (if applicable)
  • Police certificate
  • Medical examination
  • English language proficiency test results
  • Skills assessment report
  • Proof of qualifications and work experience
