United States Immigration Pathway

  • Work Visas (H-1B, L-1, O-1): Petition for a Nonimmigrant Worker (Form I-129), Labor Condition Application (LCA), and a valid passport.
  • Family Sponsorship: Petition for Alien Relative (Form I-130), Application for Immigrant Visa and Alien Registration (Form DS-260), and supporting documents like birth and marriage certificates.
  • Diversity Visa Lottery: Application through the official DV lottery website, and if selected, further documentation will be required for the visa application process.

Canada Immigration Pathway

  • Express Entry: Language test results (IELTS, CELPIP, or TEF), Educational Credential Assessment (ECA), and a valid passport.
  • Provincial Nominee Programs (PNPs): Varies by province but typically includes a nomination certificate from the province, language test results, and proof of education and work experience.
  • Family Sponsorship: Application to Sponsor a Member of the Family Class (Form IMM 1344), and the sponsored individual must apply for permanent residence using the Application for Permanent Residence: Sponsorship (Form IMM 0008).

Australia Immigration Pathway

  • Skilled Independent and Nominated Visas: Skills assessment from a relevant assessing authority, English language proficiency test results (IELTS, TOEFL, etc.), and a valid passport.
  • Family Sponsorship: Application for a sponsored visa, which requires the sponsor to be an Australian citizen, permanent resident, or eligible New Zealand citizen, and the applicant must meet health and character requirements.

Core Documentation Tracking Milestones

  1. Initial Application: Submitting the initial application or petition, which may involve online profiles, paper applications, or both.
  2. Documentation Submission: Gathering and submitting required documents, which can vary significantly depending on the pathway and country.
  3. Application Review: The period during which the immigration authorities review the application, which can include requests for additional evidence or an interview.
  4. Decision and Next Steps: Receiving a decision on the application, which if positive, leads to the next steps such as medical examinations, police certificates, and finally, the issuance of a visa or permanent resident status.
  5. Post-Arrival Compliance: For those who successfully immigrate, there may be conditions to meet, such as maintaining a certain type of employment, residing in a specific area, or applying for permanent residence after a certain period.
