United States Immigration Pathway

The United States is a top destination for immigrants seeking better opportunities. The primary pathways for immigration to the U.S. include work visas, family sponsorship, and the diversity visa lottery.

  • Work Visas (H-1B, L-1, O-1): Petition for a Nonimmigrant Worker (Form I-129), Labor Condition Application (LCA), and a valid passport.
  • Family Sponsorship: Petition for Alien Relative (Form I-130), Application for Adjustment of Status (Form I-485), and supporting documents like birth and marriage certificates.
  • Diversity Visa Lottery: Online application through the official DV lottery website, passport, and education/work experience documents.

Canada Immigration Pathway

Canada offers a points-based system for skilled workers, making it an attractive option for professionals. The core pathways include Express Entry, Provincial Nominee Programs (PNPs), and family sponsorship.

  • Express Entry: Language test results (IELTS, CELPIP, or TEF), Educational Credential Assessment (ECA), and a valid passport.
  • Provincial Nominee Programs (PNPs): Varies by province but typically includes a nomination certificate, language test results, and education/work experience documents.
  • Family Sponsorship: Application to Sponsor a Member of the Family Class (Form IMM 1344), Sponsorship Agreement and Undertaking (Form IMM 1344A), and proof of income.

Australia Immigration Pathway

Australia's immigration system is also points-based, focusing on skilled migration. The primary pathways include Skilled Independent visa (subclass 189), Skilled Nominated visa (subclass 190), and the Temporary Skilled Migration visa (subclass 457).

  • Skilled Independent and Nominated Visas: Skills assessment from a relevant assessing authority, English language proficiency test results (IELTS, TOEFL, etc.), and a valid passport.
  • Temporary Skilled Migration (TSM) visa: Nomination by an approved sponsor, skills assessment, and health insurance.
