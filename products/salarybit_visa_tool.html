Immigration to the United States

The United States is a top destination for immigrants seeking better economic opportunities, education, and political stability. The primary pathways for immigration to the U.S. include work visas, family sponsorship, and the diversity visa lottery.

Work Visas (H-1B, L-1, O-1)

These visas are for specialty occupations, intracompany transferees, and individuals with extraordinary abilities, respectively.

  • Passport
  • Degree certificates
  • Experience letters
  • Sponsorship documents from a U.S. employer

Family Sponsorship

U.S. citizens and permanent residents can sponsor family members for immigration.

  • Proof of relationship (birth or marriage certificates)
  • Sponsor's income proof
  • Medical examination records

Diversity Visa Lottery

An annual lottery for individuals from countries with low rates of immigration to the U.S.

  • Passport
  • Birth certificate
  • Marriage certificate (if applicable)
  • Police certificates

Immigration to Canada

Canada offers a points-based system for skilled workers, making it an attractive option for those looking to immigrate based on their skills and education. The primary pathways include Express Entry, Provincial Nominee Programs (PNPs), and family sponsorship.

Express Entry

A points-based system for skilled workers.

  • Language test results (English or French)
  • Educational credential assessment
  • Proof of work experience
  • Police certificates

Provincial Nominee Programs (PNPs)

Each province has its own criteria for nominating immigrants.

  • Proof of education and work experience relevant to the province's needs
  • Language proficiency test results
  • Proof of sufficient funds

Family Sponsorship

Canadian citizens and permanent residents can sponsor family members.

  • Proof of relationship
  • Sponsor's income proof
  • Medical examination records

Immigration to Australia

Australia's immigration policy focuses on skilled migration, with pathways like the Skilled Independent visa and the Skilled Nominated visa. Family sponsorship and the Temporary Skill Shortage visa are also popular options.

Skilled Independent Visa (Subclass 189)

For skilled workers who are not sponsored by an employer or family member.

  • Skills assessment for the nominated occupation
  • English language proficiency test results
  • Qualification certificates
  • Police certificates

Skilled Nominated Visa (Subclass 190)

For skilled workers nominated by an Australian state or territory.

  • Invitation to apply from the Department of Home Affairs
  • Skills assessment
  • Proof of nomination by a state or territory

Temporary Skill Shortage Visa (Subclass 482)

For employers to sponsor workers for temporary positions.

  • Sponsorship documents from the Australian employer
  • Nomination documents for the specific job
  • Skills and qualification certificates relevant to the nominated occupation
