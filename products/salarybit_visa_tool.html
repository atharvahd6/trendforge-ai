Immigration to the United States

  • Work Visas (H-1B, L-1, O-1): Requires a job offer from a U.S. employer, approval of a labor condition application, and petition approval from USCIS.
  • Study Visas (F-1, M-1): Applicants must be accepted by a U.S. school, demonstrate financial support, and apply for the visa at a U.S. embassy or consulate.
  • Family-Based Green Cards: U.S. citizens and permanent residents can sponsor family members, involving petitioning, visa application, and status adjustment.

Immigration to Canada

  • Express Entry: Points-based system ranking skilled workers based on age, education, language proficiency, and work experience.
  • Study Permits: Requires admission to a Canadian designated learning institution and proof of financial support.
  • Family Sponsorship: Canadian citizens/residents can sponsor family members with medical and background checks required.

Immigration to Australia

  • Skilled Independent Visa (Subclass 189): Points-based system for skilled workers with factors like age, English ability, and work experience.
  • Student Visa (Subclass 500): Requires offer from Australian education provider, sufficient funds, and health insurance.
  • Partner Visa (Subclass 820/801): For spouses/partners of Australian citizens/residents, with temporary and permanent stages.

Core Documentation Tracking Milestones

Application Submission
The initial step where the applicant submits their visa or immigration application.
Biometric Data Collection
Many countries require biometric data (fingerprints, facial recognition) as part of the application process.
Medical Examination
Applicants may need to undergo a medical examination to ensure they do not pose a health risk.
Background Checks
Police certificates or background checks are often required to assess the applicant's character.
Interviews
In some cases, applicants may be required to attend an interview at an embassy, consulate, or immigration office.
Visa Approval and Entry
The final step where the applicant is granted a visa and enters the destination country.
