Immigration to the United States

  • Work Visas (H-1B, L-1, O-1): Requires a job offer from a U.S. employer, approval of a labor condition application, and petition approval from U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS).
  • Study Visas (F-1, M-1): Involves acceptance into a U.S. educational institution, proof of financial support, and obtaining an I-20 form.
  • Family-Based Green Cards: Requires a sponsor who is a U.S. citizen or lawful permanent resident, filing of a petition, and going through the adjustment of status process.
  • Refugee or Asylum Status: Involves applying for refugee status outside the U.S. or seeking asylum after arriving in the U.S., with the process managed by the U.S. Refugee Admissions Program or USCIS, respectively.

Immigration to Canada

  • Express Entry for Skilled Workers: Requires creating an online profile, scoring points based on education, language proficiency, work experience, and other factors, and receiving an Invitation to Apply (ITA) for permanent residence.
  • Study Permits: Involves applying to a Canadian educational institution, receiving an acceptance letter, and applying for a study permit.
  • Family Sponsorship: Requires the sponsor to be a Canadian citizen or permanent resident, meeting income requirements, and the sponsored family member applying for permanent residence.
  • Provincial Nominee Programs (PNPs): Offers a pathway for skilled workers to immigrate based on the specific needs of Canadian provinces, with requirements varying by province.

Immigration to Australia

  • Skilled Independent Visa (Subclass 189): Requires submitting an Expression of Interest (EOI), having a skills assessment, meeting the points test, and receiving an invitation to apply.
  • Study Visas: Involves applying to an Australian educational institution, receiving a Confirmation of Enrolment (COE), and applying for a student visa.
  • Family Visas: Includes partner visas, child visas, and other family visas, requiring a sponsor who is an Australian citizen, permanent resident, or eligible New Zealand citizen.
  • Regional Sponsored Migration Scheme (RSMS): Allows employers in regional Australia to sponsor skilled workers for permanent residence, requiring a nomination by the employer and meeting specific eligibility criteria.
