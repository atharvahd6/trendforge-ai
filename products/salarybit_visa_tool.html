Immigration to the United States

The United States is a top destination for immigrants seeking better economic opportunities, education, and political stability. The primary pathways for immigration to the U.S. include work visas, family sponsorship, and the diversity visa lottery.

Work Visas (H-1B, L-1, O-1)

  • Documentation: Bachelor's degree, relevant work experience, labor condition application, and a petition from a U.S. employer.
  • Milestones:
    1. Employer Sponsorship: The U.S. employer must sponsor the applicant.
    2. Labor Condition Application (LCA): The employer files an LCA with the U.S. Department of Labor.
    3. Petition Filing: The employer files a petition (Form I-129) with U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS).
    4. Visa Application: Once the petition is approved, the applicant applies for a visa at a U.S. embassy or consulate.

Family Sponsorship

  • Documentation: Proof of family relationship, financial support documents, and a medical examination.
  • Milestones:
    1. Petition Filing: The U.S. citizen or permanent resident files a petition (Form I-130) with USCIS.
    2. Priority Date: A priority date is assigned based on the filing date of the petition.
    3. Application for Visa: Once the priority date becomes current, the applicant applies for a visa.

Diversity Visa Lottery

  • Documentation: Online application, passport, and education/work experience documents.
  • Milestones:
    1. Application Period: Applicants submit their entries during the specified annual period.
    2. Selection: Applicants are randomly selected.
    3. Interview: Selected applicants undergo an interview at a U.S. embassy or consulate.

Immigration to Canada

Canada offers a points-based system for skilled workers, making it an attractive destination for professionals. The primary pathways include Express Entry, family sponsorship, and provincial nominee programs.

Express Entry

  • Documentation: Language test results, education credential assessment, and proof of work experience.
  • Milestones:
    1. Profile Creation: Applicants create an online profile.
    2. Invitation to Apply (ITA): Candidates with high-ranking profiles receive an ITA.
    3. Permanent Residence Application: Applicants submit a complete application for permanent residence.

Family Sponsorship

  • Documentation: Proof of family relationship, financial support documents, and a medical examination.
  • Milestones:
    1. Sponsorship Application: The Canadian citizen or permanent resident applies to sponsor their family member.
    2. Permanent Residence Application: The sponsored family member applies for permanent residence.

Provincial Nominee Programs (PNPs)

  • Documentation: Varies by province but often includes education, work experience, and language proficiency.
  • Milestones:
    1. Application to PNP: Applicants apply to a specific province's program.
    2. Nomination: Successful applicants are nominated by the province.
    3. Permanent Residence Application: Nominees apply for permanent residence through the federal government.

Immigration to Australia

Australia's immigration system is also points-based, focusing on skilled migration. The primary pathways include Skilled Independent visas, family sponsorship, and state/territory nomination.

Skilled Independent Visas (Subclass 189)

  • Documentation: Skills assessment, language test results, and proof of work experience.
  • Milestones:
    1. Expression of Interest (EOI): Applicants submit an EOI through SkillSelect.
    2. Invitation to Apply: High-ranking applicants receive an invitation to apply.
    3. Visa Application: Applicants submit a visa application within the specified timeframe.

Family Sponsorship

  • Documentation: Proof of family relationship, financial support documents, and a medical examination.
  • Milestones:
    1. Sponsorship Application: The Australian citizen, permanent resident, or eligible New Zealand citizen applies to sponsor their family member.
    2. Visa Application: The sponsored family member applies for a visa.

State/Territory Nomination (Subclass 190)

  • Documentation: Similar to Skilled Independent visas, with additional requirements set by the nominating state or territory.
  • Milestones:
    1. EOI: Applicants submit an EOI indicating their interest in state/territory nomination.
    2. Nomination: Successful applicants are nominated by a state or territory.
    3. Visa Application: Nominees apply for a visa.
