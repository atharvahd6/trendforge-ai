Immigration to the United States

The United States is a top destination for immigrants due to its strong economy, cultural diversity, and educational opportunities.

  • Work Visas (H1B, L1, O1): For professionals, intra-company transferees, and individuals with extraordinary abilities.
  • Family Sponsorship: For spouses, children, parents, and siblings of U.S. citizens and permanent residents.
  • Study Visas (F1, J1): For international students and exchange visitors.

Core Documentation:

  • Passport (valid for at least 6 months beyond intended stay)
  • Visa application (DS-160 for non-immigrant visas)
  • Proof of financial support (bank statements, affidavit of support)
  • Health insurance coverage
  • Police certificate from country of residence
  • Educational transcripts and diplomas (for student and work visas)
  • Employment verification letter (for work visas)
  • SEVIS fee receipt (for student visas)

Immigration to Canada

Canada offers a welcoming environment, social services, and a points-based immigration system that attracts skilled workers, entrepreneurs, and families.

  • Express Entry: For skilled workers through the Federal Skilled Worker Program, Federal Skilled Trades Program, and Canadian Experience Class.
  • Provincial Nominee Programs (PNPs): For workers with skills needed by specific provinces.
  • Family Sponsorship: For spouses, common-law partners, children, parents, and grandparents of Canadian citizens and permanent residents.

Core Documentation:

  • Valid passport
  • Language test results (IELTS for English or TEF for French)
  • Educational credential assessment (WES or other designated organizations)
  • Police certificates from all countries resided in for 6+ months
  • Medical exam from a panel physician
  • Proof of funds (bank statements, investment documents)
  • Job offer letter (if applicable)
  • Express Entry profile number (if applicable)
  • Provincial nomination certificate (if applicable)

Immigration to Australia

Australia is known for its high standard of living, diverse culture, and points-based immigration system, making it an attractive destination for immigrants.

  • Skilled Migration (Subclass 189, 190): For skilled workers who can contribute to the Australian economy.
  • Work Visas (Subclass 482, 417): For temporary skilled workers and working holiday makers.
  • Family Sponsorship: For partners, children, parents, and other family members of Australian citizens, permanent residents, and eligible New Zealand citizens.

Core Documentation:

  • Valid passport
  • Skills assessment from relevant assessing authority
  • English language proficiency test results (IELTS, PTE, or TOEFL)
  • Qualifications and academic transcripts
  • Police certificates from all countries resided in for 12+ months
  • Health insurance coverage
  • Proof of sufficient funds
  • Employment reference letters
  • Expression of Interest (EOI) submission proof
  • State or territory nomination (if applicable)
